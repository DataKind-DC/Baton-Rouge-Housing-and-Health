# Most Interesting Variable Combinations for Analysis

## Cross-Variable Insights: What Makes a Great Story?

This guide identifies the most revealing variable combinations that tell compelling stories about Cook County communities.

---

## üèÜ TOP 10 MOST REVEALING COMPARISONS

### 1. The Gentrification Detector
**Primary Layer:** Median_Home_Value
**Compare with:**
- Pct_Black / Pct_White (shows racial change)
- Moved_In_2020_Later (shows new arrivals)
- Pct_Renter_Occupied (owners vs renters)
- Pct_Bachelors_Plus (education level rising)
- Built_2020_Later (new construction)

**What it reveals:** Neighborhoods undergoing rapid change. High home values + recent move-ins + changing demographics = gentrification pressure

---

### 2. The Veteran Struggle Index
**Primary Layer:** Vet_Total_Veterans
**Compare with:**
- Pct_Veterans_Unemployed (job struggles)
- Pct_With_VA_HealthCare (healthcare access)
- Vet_Median_Income_Veterans (economic wellbeing)
- Pct_Veterans_With_Service_Disability (health challenges)
- High_Rent_Burden_50_Plus_Units (housing affordability)

**What it reveals:** Where veterans concentrate and face challenges. Shows if veteran-dense areas have adequate support systems

---

### 3. The Healthcare Desert Detector
**Primary Layer:** Pct_No_Health_Insurance
**Compare with:**
- Pct_Hispanic (demographic factor)
- Pct_Below_Poverty (economic factor)
- Pct_With_DirectPurchase_Insurance (marketplace uptake)
- With_VA_HealthCare (alternative care)
- Median_Household_Income (affordability)

**What it reveals:** Why people lack insurance and what alternatives they use. Hispanic communities often show high uninsured rates despite marketplace availability

---

### 4. The Child Poverty Trap
**Primary Layer:** Children_Below_Poverty
**Compare with:**
- Children_In_Single_Mother_Families (family structure)
- Pct_HH_SNAP (food assistance needs)
- Three_Plus_Bedroom_Units (housing size mismatch?)
- Pct_No_Health_Insurance (healthcare access)
- Pct_Households_With_Children (concentration of families)

**What it reveals:** Multi-dimensional challenges facing children. Single-parent families + SNAP + no insurance = severe hardship

---

### 5. The Commuter's Burden
**Primary Layer:** Pct_Commute_60_Plus
**Compare with:**
- Pct_HH_No_Vehicle (car access)
- Median_Home_Value (affordability trade-off)
- Pct_Owner_Occupied (homeownership pushes to suburbs)
- Population_per_sqmi (urban vs suburban)
- Median_Household_Income (can they afford closer housing?)

**What it reveals:** Job-housing mismatch. Long commutes often mean housing affordability forces people to live far from jobs

---

### 6. The Digital Divide Reality
**Primary Layer:** Pct_HH_With_Broadband
**Compare with:**
- Pct_HH_No_Computer (device access)
- Pct_Below_Poverty (economic barrier)
- Pct_Bachelors_Plus (education correlation)
- Pct_Work_At_Home (remote work capability)
- Children_Below_Poverty (education access for kids)

**What it reveals:** Technology access barriers. Lack of broadband + no computers + poverty = locked out of digital economy

---

### 7. The Affordable Housing Crisis Map
**Primary Layer:** High_Rent_Burden_50_Plus_Units
**Compare with:**
- Median_Gross_Rent (absolute cost)
- Median_Household_Income (income levels)
- Pct_Renter_Occupied (renter concentration)
- Moved_In_2020_Later (recent arrivals paying new rates)
- Pct_HH_SNAP (economic desperation)

**What it reveals:** Where renters spend more than half their income on housing. Often shows disconnect between housing costs and local wages

---

### 8. The Multigenerational Survival Strategy
**Primary Layer:** Pct_Multigenerational
**Compare with:**
- Median_Household_Income (economic pressure)
- Three_Plus_Bedroom_Units (space needs)
- Pct_Hispanic / Pct_Asian (cultural patterns)
- Grandparents_Responsible_For_Grandchildren (caregiving)
- Below_Poverty_Level (economic necessity)

**What it reveals:** Where families combine households. Can be cultural preference OR economic necessity. Context matters!

---

### 9. The Education-Income Correlation
**Primary Layer:** Pct_Bachelors_Plus
**Compare with:**
- Median_Household_Income (economic payoff)
- Median_Home_Value (neighborhood investment)
- Pct_With_Employer_Insurance (job quality)
- Pct_Below_Poverty (inverse relationship)
- Pct_HH_With_Broadband (tech access)

**What it reveals:** Stark education-based inequality. High education = high income, low poverty, better everything

---

### 10. The Veteran vs Civilian Divide
**Primary Layer:** Pct_Veterans_Of_Adult_Pop
**Compare with:**
- Vet_Median_Income_Veterans vs Median_Household_Income
- Pct_Employed_With_Insurance vs Pct_With_Health_Insurance (employment-based coverage vs overall)
- Vet_Education_Bachelors_Plus vs Pct_Bachelors_Plus
- Pct_With_VA_HealthCare + Pct_With_TRICARE (military healthcare)
- Pct_Owner_Occupied (homeownership rates)

**What it reveals:** Are veterans doing better or worse than civilians in same neighborhoods?

## üîç SURPRISING PATTERNS TO LOOK FOR

### Pattern 1: The Wealth Without Ownership Paradox
**Variables:** High Median_Household_Income BUT Low Pct_Owner_Occupied
**Common in:** Urban high-rise areas, young professional neighborhoods
**Insight:** High earners choosing to rent (or unable to buy due to high prices)

---

### Pattern 2: The Car-Free But Long Commute Mystery
**Variables:** High Pct_HH_No_Vehicle AND High Pct_Commute_60_Plus
**Common in:** Low-income urban areas with poor transit
**Insight:** People without cars suffering long transit commutes = transit desert

---

### Pattern 3: The Insured But Poor Puzzle
**Variables:** Low Pct_No_Health_Insurance BUT High Pct_Below_Poverty
**Common in:** Areas with high Medicaid enrollment
**Insight:** Safety net working - poor people getting Medicaid coverage

---

### Pattern 4: The Education But No Income Disconnect
**Variables:** High Pct_Bachelors_Plus BUT Low Median_Household_Income
**Common in:** University neighborhoods, artist communities
**Insight:** Educated but underemployed - adjuncts, artists, students

---

### Pattern 5: The Veteran Healthcare Gap
**Variables:** High Vet_Total_Veterans BUT Low Pct_With_VA_HealthCare
**Common in:** Veteran communities far from VA facilities
**Insight:** Veterans eligible but not using VA care (access, quality, or other insurance)

---

### Pattern 6: The Multigenerational Wealth Split
**Variables:** High Pct_Multigenerational in both high-income AND low-income tracts
**Pattern:** Bimodal distribution
**Insight:** Cultural choice in affluent areas, economic necessity in poor areas

Map concentric rings from downtown showing:
- Density decreasing
- Home values (U-shaped: high downtown, low middle ring, high far suburbs)

- Commute times (U-shaped: short downtown, long middle, medium far suburbs)

Many Chicago patterns split North vs South:
- Income inequality

- Health outcomes
- Educational attainment

- Home values (premium near stations)
- Car ownership (lower near transit)
- Commute patterns
- Density patterns

### Lakefront Premium:
Eastern edge (Lake Michigan) typically shows:
- Higher home values
- Higher incomes
- Higher education
- Better health outcomes

## üé® VISUALIZATION BEST PRACTICES

### Diverging Color Scales (Good vs Bad):
- Use for: Poverty, uninsured rate, rent burden
- Scale: Green (good) ‚Üí Yellow (medium) ‚Üí Red (bad)

### Sequential Scales (More is More):
- Use for: Population, veterans, income, education
- Scale: Light ‚Üí Dark in single color

### Categorical Colors (Groups):
- Use for: Predominant race/ethnicity
- Scale: Distinct colors for each category

### Quartile Breaks:
- Show: Top 25%, Middle 50%, Bottom 25%
- Good for: Identifying extremes

### Natural Breaks (Jenks):
- Use: When data has natural clusters
- Good for: Finding neighborhood types
